# CyberPath Academy — 30-Day Market Execution Plan

CyberPath Academy should earn school/cohort readiness through real pilots, retention, safety trust, and willingness-to-pay evidence. This plan deliberately prioritizes customer learning over vanity feature work.

## North-Star Validation Metrics

By day 30, target:

- **100 demo users** across students, teachers, mentors, parents, and school leaders.
- **30 feedback submissions** tied to lessons, labs, onboarding, demo exit, and artifact creation.
- **10 interviews** with students, mentors, teachers, parents, or learning-center operators.
- **3 school pilot conversations** with actual decision-makers or program owners.
- **1 paid or serious pilot commitment** with clear cohort size, timeline, success criteria, and decision owner.

## Week 1 — Demo, Pilot Capture, and First Outreach

Goals:

- Polish the public demo so a visitor understands the school/cohort positioning in under 10 seconds.
- Create and verify the pilot form.
- Prepare a short teacher/learning-center pitch.
- Contact **20 students** and **5 teachers/mentors**.

Execution checklist:

1. Test the student, mentor, and admin demo paths on mobile and laptop.
2. Confirm the school pilot form captures contact name, email, phone/Telegram, role, organization, city/country, student count, age range, current cyber level, needs, interest level, willingness to pay, and message.
3. Prepare a one-page teacher pitch:
   - Defensive-only cybersecurity education.
   - Fictional labs only.
   - Cohort dashboard and student reports.
   - Portfolio proof-of-work.
   - Beta pilot expectations.
4. Send targeted outreach to students and teachers in cyber clubs, schools, tutoring centers, and learning communities.
5. Log every conversation as either: not interested, curious, interested, ready for pilot, or paid-pilot candidate.

Week 1 success threshold:

- 25 people contacted.
- 10 demo sessions started.
- 5 feedback submissions.
- 2 serious teacher/mentor conversations.

## Week 2 — First User Tests and Product Fixes

Goals:

- Run the first **10 student tests**.
- Collect confusion and willingness-to-pay data.
- Fix the top **5 product issues**.

Execution checklist:

1. Watch 10 students use the demo without coaching.
2. Ask after every session:
   - What confused you?
   - Which lab felt useful?
   - Would you show this to a teacher or parent?
   - Would you pay, or would your school pay?
3. Use feedback analytics to identify:
   - Most confusing lessons.
   - Most useful labs.
   - Low-scoring labs.
   - Drop-off before pilot form.
4. Fix the top 5 issues blocking comprehension, safety trust, or pilot conversion.
5. Re-test the fixed flow with 3 additional users if possible.

Week 2 success threshold:

- 10 observed student tests.
- 15 cumulative feedback submissions.
- 5 concrete product fixes.
- 1 school/learning-center follow-up call scheduled.

## Week 3 — Mini Cohort Test

Goals:

- Run a mini cohort with **10–20 learners**.
- Collect completion, lab, and artifact data.
- Interview **3 teachers/mentors**.

Execution checklist:

1. Create a 1-week defensive learning sprint:
   - 2 starter lessons.
   - 1 fictional lab.
   - 1 proof-of-work artifact.
   - 1 short reflection.
2. Use the mentor dashboard to monitor:
   - Active students this week.
   - Inactive students.
   - Average lesson completion.
   - Average quiz accuracy.
   - Labs submitted.
   - Portfolio artifacts created.
   - Weakest topics.
3. Export a CSV cohort report and show it to a teacher/mentor.
4. Interview 3 teachers/mentors about safety, reporting, content clarity, and purchase process.
5. Identify whether schools care more about cyber club engagement, parent trust, job readiness, competition prep, or English/Uzbek accessibility.

Week 3 success threshold:

- 10–20 learners enrolled in a mini cohort.
- 60%+ complete at least one lesson.
- 40%+ submit at least one lab.
- 25%+ create at least one artifact.
- 3 teacher/mentor interviews completed.

## Week 4 — Pilot Offers and Commercial Decision

Goals:

- Offer a pilot to **3 schools or learning centers**.
- Define the first paid school package.
- Collect testimonials only if real and permissioned.
- Decide whether to continue, pivot, or narrow.

Execution checklist:

1. Pitch 3 schools/learning centers with:
   - Pilot cohort size.
   - Duration.
   - Safety promise.
   - Mentor reporting.
   - Student artifacts.
   - Support expectations.
   - Introductory pilot price or signed serious commitment.
2. Define the first School/Cohort package:
   - Student seats.
   - Mentor/admin seats.
   - Duration.
   - Included support.
   - Reporting cadence.
   - Success criteria.
3. Collect real testimonials only from users who actually used the product and gave permission.
4. Review metrics and make the hard decision:
   - Continue if schools show urgency and willingness to pay.
   - Narrow if only one segment responds strongly.
   - Pivot if users like the idea but no one commits time, trust, or money.

Week 4 success threshold:

- 3 school pilot conversations.
- 1 paid or serious pilot commitment.
- Clear price/package hypothesis.
- Decision memo: continue, pivot, or narrow.

## Brutal Operating Rule

Do not add more content until the pilot funnel produces evidence. The next month is about proving that schools, mentors, and learners trust the safe defensive model enough to use it repeatedly and pay for cohort accountability.
