# Teacher Pilot Pitch

## What CyberPath Academy is

CyberPath Academy is a defensive-only cybersecurity learning platform for students, cyber clubs, mentors, and small cohorts. Students learn concepts, practice safe scenarios, complete fictional labs, and create portfolio artifacts that teachers can review.

## Why schools need it

Schools want cybersecurity education, but many resources are too unsafe, too advanced, or impossible for non-specialist teachers to supervise. CyberPath focuses on structured defensive learning, safe fictional datasets, and teacher visibility.

## What students do

- Complete onboarding and a beginner diagnostic.
- Learn foundational cybersecurity concepts.
- Answer practice and review questions.
- Submit fictional defensive labs.
- Create artifacts such as incident notes, access reviews, risk registers, and executive summaries.

## What teachers see

- Cohort progress and activity.
- Weak topics and students needing help.
- Lab submissions and rubric-style feedback.
- Portfolio artifacts for proof of work.
- Assignment and follow-up signals.

## Safety promise

CyberPath Academy does not ask students to attack real systems. Labs are fictional, defensive, and authorization-focused. Students are repeatedly told not to scan, exploit, test, or investigate real targets.

## Pilot structure

- Size: 10–30 students.
- Duration: 2–4 weeks.
- Required activity: 1 lesson, 1 practice set, 1 lab, 1 artifact, and feedback.
- Teacher activity: review dashboard, inspect student report, review at least one artifact, and complete pilot feedback.

## Pricing experiment

Pilot conversations should test whether schools prefer:

- Free 2-week pilot, then per-cohort fee.
- Per-student monthly price.
- School/club license including mentor dashboard.
- Learning center package with setup support.
