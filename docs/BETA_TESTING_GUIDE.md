# CyberPath Academy Beta Testing Guide

CyberPath Academy is ready for a **controlled beta**, not a paid public launch. The beta should prove whether students and teachers can use the product without founder explanation.

## Safety rules

- Use only fictional labs, toy datasets, and authorized demo accounts.
- Do not scan, probe, exploit, or test any real website, network, account, person, or organization.
- Do not paste real student data, credentials, private messages, or school incidents into the product.
- Report any content that feels like offensive hacking guidance.

## How to test as a student

1. Create or use a student demo account.
2. Complete onboarding and diagnostic questions.
3. Open the first recommended lesson and mark it complete.
4. Complete one practice or review session.
5. Submit one safe lab using fictional evidence.
6. Create one portfolio artifact from the lab.
7. Submit feedback from the feedback checkpoint or support page.

## How to test as a teacher or mentor

1. Sign in as a mentor/admin demo account.
2. Open the mentor dashboard.
3. Review cohort progress, weak topics, student risk status, lab submissions, and artifacts.
4. Decide whether the dashboard tells you what to do next with a real cohort.
5. Submit school pilot interest and mentor-dashboard feedback.

## What feedback to give

- What page confused you?
- What step did you abandon?
- Was the lesson/practice/lab useful from 1–5?
- Was the difficulty too easy, right level, or too hard?
- Would you recommend it to another student/teacher?
- Would you or your school pay for this if improved?
- What broke technically?

## What to ignore during beta

- Enterprise SSO, full payment workflows, polished content library breadth, perfect localization, and advanced labs.
- The goal is one complete learning loop, teacher comprehension, useful feedback, and pilot demand.
